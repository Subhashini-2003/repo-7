Recipe 1: Biryani

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 pound meat (chicken, mutton, or fish), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
    •  
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 4 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil
  • salt to taste
  • A few strands of saffron, soaked in 2 tablespoons of warm milk (optional, for color and flavor)
  • water

Process:

Step 1:Prepare the Rice:Wash the basmati rice thoroughly under running water until the water runs clear.Soak the rice in enough water to cover it for at least 30 minutes. Drain the water before cooking.

Step 2:Marinate the Meat:In a large, turmeric powder, red chili powder, cumin powder, garam masala, yogurt, mint leaves, cilantro, and salt.Mix well and marinate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 4 hours in the refrigerator.

Step 3:Cook the Meat:Heat ghee or oil in a heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium heat.Add the sliced onions and fry until golden brown and crisp. Remove half of the fried onions and set aside for layering.Add the marinated meat to the pot and cook over medium-high heat until it is browned on all sides.Add 1/2 cup of water, cover the pot, and cook over low heat until the meat is cooked through and tender. This may take 20-30 minutes, depending on the type of meat. Make sure there is some gravy left in the pot.

Step 4:Cook the Rice:While the meat is cooking, bring 4 cups of water to a boil in a separate pot. Add salt to taste.Add the drained rice to the boiling water and cook until it is about 70-80% cooked. The rice should still have a slight bite to it.Drain the rice and set aside.

step 5:Layer the Biryani:In the pot with the cooked meat, add a layer of half of the partially cooked rice.   Sprinkle some of the reserved fried onions, chopped mint, and cilantro over the rice.Add the remaining rice on top.Pour the saffron milk over the rice (if using).Garnish with the remaining fried onions, mint, ancilantro.

step 6:Dum Cooking (Steaming):Cover the pot tightly with a lid. You can seal the edges of the lid with dough or aluminum foil to trap the steam.Cook the biryani on low heat for 15-20 minutes. This process, called "dum cooking," allows the flavors to meld together.Alternatively, you can preheat an oven to 300°F (150°C) and bake the covered pot for 20-25 minutes.

Recipe 3 tomato rice

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice (basmati or any other long-grain rice)
  • 2-3 ripe tomatoes, finely chopped or pureed
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
    •  
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
    •  
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
    •  
  • 1/4 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • A few curry leaves
    •  
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, for garnish

Process:

Step 1:Wash the rice thoroughly and soak it for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.Heat oil or ghee in a pot or pressure cooker. Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds. Let them splutter.Add curry leaves and green chilies (if using). Saute for a few seconds.Add sliced onions and saute until they turn golden brown.

Step 2:Add ginger-garlic paste and saute for a minute until the raw smell disappears.Add chopped or pureed tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until the tomatoes are mushy and the oil starts to separate from the mixture.Add garam masala and mix well.Add the soaked and drained rice. Mix gently to coat the rice with the tomato mixture.

Step 3:Add water according to the rice-to-water ratio for your chosen cooking method (usually 2 cups of water for 1 cup of rice if cooking in a pot, slightly less if using a pressure cooker).Bring the water to a boil. Cover the pot and simmer on low heat until all the water is absorbed and the rice is cooked. If using a pressure cooker, close the lid and cook for one whistle on medium heat. Let the pressure release naturally. Once the rice is cooked, gently fluff it with a fork

step 4:Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.Serve hot with raita (yogurt with vegetables), papad, or a simple curry.

Recipe 5 pulao

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups Basmati rice (long-grain)
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans, potatoes, cauliflower, etc.), chopped
  • 1/2 cup paneer (Indian cottage cheese) cubes
  • 1 bay leaf
    •  
  • 1 inch cinnamon stick
  • 3-4 cloves
  • 3-4 cardamom pods
    •  
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
    •  
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    •  
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • salt to taste.
  • 2 tablespoons fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 2-3 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil
  • 2 1/2 to 3 cups water (adjust based on rice type)

Process:

Step 1:Rice Preparation:Rinse the basmati rice thoroughly under running water until the water runs clear.Soak the rice in water for 20-30 minutes. This helps the rice cook evenly and become fluffy.Drain the soaked rice.

Step 2:Tempering the Spices:Heat ghee or oil in a heavy-bottomed pot or pressure cooker.Add the whole spices (bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom, cumin seeds) and let them splutter.Add the sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.Add the ginger garlic paste, and saute for another minute.

Step 3:Cooking Vegetables:Add the chopped vegetables and sauté for a few minutes until they are slightly tender.If using paneer, add the paneer and lightly fry it.

step 4:Adding Rice and Spices:Add the drained rice to the pot and mix gently, ensuring the rice is coated with the spices and vegetables.Add the turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well.

step 5:Adding Water and Cooking:Pour in the water. The general rule is to use a 1:1.5 or 1:2 ratio of rice to water, but adjust based on your rice type.Bring the mixture to a boil.Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot tightly, and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and the water is absorbed.   If using a pressure cooker, close the lid and cook for 1-2 whistles on medium heat. Let the pressure release naturally.

step 6:Garnishing and Serving:Once cooked, gently fluff the rice with a fork.Garnish with chopped mint and coriander leaves.Serve the pulao hot with raita or a side dish of your choice.

Recipe 6 veg manchuria

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup finely chopped mixed vegetables (cabbage, carrots, beans, bell peppers)
  • 2-3 tablespoons all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2-3 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for deep frying
  • For the Manchurian Sauce:
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped ginger
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped garlic
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 1 bell pepper (capsicum), diced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1-2 tablespoons tomato ketchup
    •  
  • 1 tablespoon red chili sauce
  • 1 teaspoon vinegar
  • 1 cup vegetable broth or water
  • 1-2 tablespoons cornstarch mixed with 2-3 tablespoons water (cornstarch slurry)
  • Spring onion greens, for garnish

Process:

Step 1:Preparing the Vegetable Balls:In a large bowl, combine the finely chopped vegetables, ginger-garlic paste, salt, and pepper. Add the all-purpose flour and cornstarch. Mix well until the vegetables are coated and the mixture can be formed into small balls. If the mixture is too dry, add a little water. Heat oil in a deep frying pan or wok.Shape the vegetable mixture into small balls.Deep fry the balls until golden brown and crispy.Drain the fried balls on paper towels to remove excess oil.

Step 2:Preparing the Manchurian Sauce:Heat oil in a wok or pan.Add the chopped ginger, garlic, and green chilies. Sauté for a minute until fragrant.Add the diced onion and bell pepper. Sauté until the onions are translucent.Add the soy sauce, tomato ketchup, red chili sauce, and vinegar. Mix well. Pour in the vegetable broth or water and bring to a simmer.Add the cornstarch slurry to thicken the sauce. Stir continuously to prevent lumps.Simmer the sauce until it thickens to your desired consistency

Step 3:Combining the Balls and Sauce:Add the fried vegetable balls to the Manchurian sauce.Toss gently to coat the balls evenly with the sauce.Cook for a few minutes until the balls are heated through.

Step 4:Garnishing and Serving:Garnish with chopped spring onion greens.Serve hot with fried rice or noodles.

Recipe 8 pulihora

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups rice (short-grain rice is preferred)
    •  
  • Water, as needed
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric powder
  • Lemon-sized ball of tamarind
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 3-4 tablespoons oil (sesame oil is traditional)
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    •  
  • 1 tablespoon chana dal (split chickpeas)
    •  
  • 1 tablespoon urad dal (split black gram)
    •  
  • 1/4 cup peanuts
    •  
  • 2-3 dried red chilies, broken
    •  
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
    •  
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
    •  
  • 1/4 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
    •  
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
    •  
  • Optional: A small piece of jaggery

Process:

Step 1:Prepare the Rice:Rinse the rice thoroughly.Cook the rice until it's done, but make sure it's not mushy. Grains should be separate. Add turmeric powder to the rice water while cooking to give the rice a nice yellow tint.Spread the cooked rice on a wide plate to cool completely.

Step 2:Extract Tamarind Pulp:Soak the tamarind in warm water for about 15-20 minutes.Squeeze the tamarind to extract the thick pulp. Strain it to remove any fibers or seeds.

Step 3:Prepare the Tamarind Mixture:Heat the oil in a pan.Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. When they splutter, add chana dal, urad dal, and peanuts. Fry until the lentils turn golden brown.Add dried red chilies, green chilies, curry leaves, and asafoetida. Sauté for a few seconds.Add the tamarind pulp, turmeric powder, and salt. If you're using jaggery, add it now.Cook the tamarind mixture until it thickens and the oil separates. Stir occasionally to prevent it from sticking to the pan.Allow this mixture to cool down.

step 4:Combine Rice and Tamarind Mixture:Once the tamarind mixture and the rice have cooled, gently mix them together.Ensure the rice is evenly coated with the tamarind mixture.

step 5:Serve the pulihora at room temperature. It's often accompanied by papad or pickles.

Recipe 10 frish fry

Ingredients:

  • 500g fish fillets (cod, tilapia, catfish, or any firm white fish)
  • Cut into desired size pieces
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice or vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour or rice flour (for a crispier coating)
  • 1/2 cup of corn flour to add to the all purpose flour
  • bread crumbs can be added for extra crispiness.
  • Enough oil for deep frying

Process:

Step 1:Marinate the Fish:In a bowl, combine the ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala (if using), and salt.Coat the fish pieces thoroughly with the marinade.Let the fish marinate for at least 30 minutes, or longer for more intense flavor.

Step 2:Prepare the Coating:In a shallow dish, spread the flour or rice flour. If you are using corn flour or bread crumbs, mix them in with the flour.

Step 3:Coat the Fish:Take each marinated fish piece and coat it evenly with the flour mixture.Ensure the fish is completely covered.

step 4:Fry the Fish:Heat the oil in a deep frying pan or wok over medium heat.Carefully place the coated fish pieces into the hot oil. Fry until golden brown and crispy on all sides.Remove the fried fish and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.

Recipe 11 mutton fry

Ingredients:

  • 500g mutton, cut into small, bite-sized pieces (boneless or with small bones)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1-2 teaspoons red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala (optional)
  • 2-3 tablespoons oil (vegetable or coconut oil)
  • Salt to taste
  • Curry leaves and green chilies for added flavor during the final fry.

Process:

Step 1:Marinate the Mutton:In a bowl, combine the mutton pieces with ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt.Mix thoroughly, ensuring the mutton is well-coated.Let the mutton marinate for at least 30 minutes, or longer for a deeper flavor.

Step 2:Initial Cooking (Tenderizing):This step is crucial for tender mutton. You can use either a pressure cooker or a heavy-bottomed pot.Pressure Cooker: Add the marinated mutton to the pressure cooker with a very small amount of water (just enough to prevent burning). Cook for 3-4 whistles, or until the mutton is tender.Pot: Add the marinated mutton to the pot with a small amount of water. Cover and simmer over low heat until the mutton is tender. This will take longer than pressure cooking.Once the mutton is tender, if there is remaining water in the pot or pressure cooker, cook it off over medium heat until all the liquid evaporates.

Step 3:The Actual Fry:Heat the oil in a pan or wok over medium-high heat.Add the cooked mutton pieces to the hot oil.Fry the mutton, stirring frequently, until it is well-browned and crispy on the edges.Optional: in the last few minutes of frying, add curry leaves and slit green chilies to the pan, and fry them with the mutton.If using, sprinkle the garam masala over the fried mutton and mix well.

Recipe 12 paneer biryani

Ingredients:

  • 250g paneer, cubed
  • 1/2 cup yogurt (curd)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    •  
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
    •  
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 1/2 cups basmati rice
  • Whole spices (2 bay leaves, 2-3 cardamom pods, 3-4 cloves, 1-inch cinnamon stick)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 medium onions, thinly sliced
    •  
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/4 cup mint leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 cup coriander leaves, chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • Saffron strands soaked in warm milk (optional)
    •  
  • Ghee or oil
    •  
  • Fried onions (birista) for garnish (optional)

Process:

Step 1:Marinate the Paneer:In a bowl, combine yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Add the paneer cubes and mix gently.Marinate for at least 30 minutes.

Step 2:Prepare the Rice:Rinse the basmati rice and soak it for 20-30 minutes.In a pot, bring water to a boil. Add the whole spices and salt.Drain the soaked rice and add it to the boiling water.Cook the rice until it's about 70-80% done. Drain and set aside.

Step 3:Prepare the Biryani Base:Heat ghee or oil in a heavy-bottomed pot.Add the sliced onions and fry until golden brown.Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften.Add the marinated paneer and cook for a few minutes.add the chopped mint and coriander leaves, and the slit green chillies 

step 4:Layer the Biryani:Layer half of the cooked rice over the paneer mixture.Sprinkle some fried onions (if using) and saffron milk (if using) over the rice.Layer the remaining rice over the top.Cover the pot tightly.

step 5:Cook on Dum:Cook the biryani on low heat (dum) for 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is fully cooked.Alternatively, you can place the pot in a preheated oven.

step 6:Garnish and Serve:Gently fluff the biryani with a fork.Garnish with remaining fried onions and coriander leaves.Serve hot with raita.

Recipe 14 soya keema masala

    •

  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped

  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped

    •

    •

    •

    •

    •

Recipe 15 veg noodles

Ingredients:

  • 200g noodles (hakka noodles, chow mein noodles, or any long noodles)
  • 2-3 tablespoons oil (vegetable or sesame)
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 bell pepper (capsicum), julienned
  • 1/2 cup cabbage, shredded
  • 1/2 cup beans, cut into small pieces
    •  
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit (optional)
    •  
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon chili sauce (optional)
    •  
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Spring onion greens, chopped (for garnish)

Process:

Step 1:Boil water in a large pot.Add the noodles and cook according to package instructions, until al dente. Do not overcook.Drain the noodles and rinse them with cold water to stop the cooking process and prevent them from sticking together.Set aside.

Step 2:Wash and cut all the vegetables into julienne strips or thin slices.

Step 3:Heat oil in a wok or large frying pan over high heat.Add the minced garlic and grated ginger. Sauté for a few seconds until fragrant.If using, add the slit green chilies.

Step 5:Add the cooked noodles to the wok.Pour in the soy sauce, vinegar, and chili sauce (if using).Toss everything well to coat the noodles and vegetables with the sauces.Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Process:

Step 1:Prepare Eggs & RiceBoil Eggs: Hard boil the eggs (boil for 8-10 minutes). Peel once cooled and keep aside. You can make light slits on them if desired.Soak Rice: Gently rinse the Basmati rice 2-3 times. Soak in water for 15-20 minutes. Drain. Cook Rice: In a pot, add the 2 cups of water, 1/2 tsp salt, and the optional bay leaf/cinnamon. Bring to a boil. Add the drained rice. Cook covered on medium-low heat until the rice is about 80-90% cooked (most water absorbed, rice grains tender but with a slight bite). Remove from heat. If there's any excess water, drain it carefully.

Step 2:Make Simple Egg Masala:While the rice cooks, heat oil/ghee in a separate pan or a heavy-bottomed pot (which can be used for dum later).Add sliced/chopped onions and sauté until light golden brown (about 5-7 minutes).Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute until the raw smell goes.If using tomatoes, add them now and cook until softened (2-3 minutes). To make the seal better for a "dum" effect: If your lid has a vent, you can plug it with a small piece of dough or paper. You can place a heavy weight on the lid.Alternatively, quickly press a layer of aluminum foil over the pot mouth before putting on the lid if you have it.Cook on the absolute lowest heat for 10-15 minutes. This allows the flavors to steam and meld. Avoid opening the lid.

Step 4:Rest & Serve:Turn off the heat and let the biryani sit covered for at least 5-10 minutes. Gently fluff the biryani from the sides.Garnish with optional chopped coriander leaves.Serve hot, as is, or with a simple side of plain yogurt or sliced onions.

Recipe 30 snail curry

Ingredients:

  • 1 teaspoon Mustard Seeds
  • 1 teaspoon Cumin Seeds (Jeera)
  • 1 sprig Curry Leaves
  • 2 Green Chillies, slit
  • 1 large Onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric Powder
  • 1-2 teaspoons Red Chilli Powder
  • 1 teaspoon Coriander Powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon Cumin Powder
  • 1/2 - 1 teaspoon Garam Masala
  • 2 medium Tomatoes, finely chopped or pureed
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup Water, or as needed
  • Fresh Coriander Leaves, chopped

Process:

Step 1:Tempering (Popu/Tadka):Heat oil in a heavy-bottomed pan or kadai over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add mustard seeds. Let them splutter.Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle. Add the curry leaves and slit green chillies. Sauté for a few seconds until the curry leaves turn crisp and aromatic.

Step 2:Sauté Aromatics:Add the finely chopped onions to the pan. Sauté until they turn translucent or light golden brown (about 7-10 minutes).Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.

Step 3:Add Spices Add turmeric powder, red chilli powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder (if using). Sauté on low heat for about a minute, stirring continuously, until the spices are fragrant. Be careful not to burn them.

Step 4:Cook Tomatoes:Add the chopped tomatoes (or tomato puree) to the pan. Cook until the tomatoes soften and the oil starts to separate from the masala (about 5-7 minutes). If using tamarind pulp, you can add its extract now.

Step 5:Cook the Snails:Add the cleaned snail meat to the masala. Mix well to coat the snails with the spices.Sauté for 5-7 minutes, allowing the snails to absorb the flavors.Add salt to taste and about 1/2 cup of water (or as needed to achieve your desired gravy consistency). Stir well.

Step 6:Cook the Snails:Add the cleaned snail meat to the masala. Mix well to coat the snails with the spices.Sauté for 5-7 minutes, allowing the snails to absorb the flavors.Add salt to taste and about 1/2 cup of water (or as needed to achieve your desired gravy consistency). Stir well.

Step 7:Finish with Garam Masala & Optional Ingredients:Once the snails are tender and the gravy has thickened to your liking, stir in the garam masala powder.If using coconut milk, add it at this stage and simmer gently for another 2-3 minutes. Do not boil vigorously after adding coconut milk.If using lemon juice (and not tamarind earlier), stir it in after turning off the heat.Check seasoning and adjust salt or spices if needed.

Step 8:Garnish and Serve:Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.Serve hot Snail Curry with steamed rice, idli, dosa, or chapati. In Andhra, it's commonly eaten with rice or ragi sangati.
